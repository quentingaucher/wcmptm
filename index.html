50 Years of Change

A look at LGBT civil rights over the last half-century of state legislation changes. Documenting laws and regulations that both restrict and promote LGBT rights.

Over the last fifty years, there has been a marked increase in the sheer quantity of state legislation that deals with Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender rights. The Supreme Court struck down DOMA and Prop 8 in June 2013, which has since had a significant impact at the state and federal level. It is worth taking a look at how state LGBT laws addressing marriage, adoption, housing, hate crimes, hospital visitation, and employment have changed over the last half-century, especially in recent years. About This Map

"Fifty Years of Change" documents the laws the United States has passed in the half-century that both restrict and extend LGBT rights. Over the last fifty years, there has been a marked increase in the sheer quantity of legislation that deals with LGBT rights. The spikes in the number of LGBT-related laws are sometimes grouped in time by the fact that they restrict rights for LGBT individuals, while at other times the spikes consist mostly of laws that protect and extend LBGT rights.

By looking at LBGT rights through time one cannot deny that a fundamental conversation is currently underway in our country, and has been exponentially growing to this point. So why document the last fifty years now? The Supreme Court struck down DOMA and Prop 8 in June 2013, which has since had a significant impact on the state and federal level. We believe it is important to review our nation's journey in LGBT civil rights and to look forward to where state laws are heading.
